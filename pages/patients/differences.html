Differentiating between Normal, Prediabetes
& Diabetes

Doctors can determine whether a patient has a normal metabolism, prediabetes or diabetes
in one of three different ways:

  1. The FPG (Fasting Plasma Glucose) test

    • at least 7 mmol/l means DIABETES
    • between 5.6 and 6.9 mmol/l means PREDIABETES
    • less than 5.6 mmol/l means NORMAL

  2. The OGTT (Oral Glucose Tolerance Test)

    • at least 11.1 mmol/l means DIABETES
    • between 7.8 and 11.0 mmol/l means PREDIABETES
    • less than 7.8 mmol/l means NORMAL

  3. The HbA1C (A1C or Glycosylated Heamoglobin) test

    • at least 6.5% means DIABETES
    • between 5.7% and 6.4% means PREDIABETES
    • less than 5.7% means NORMAL

If i have signs, what should i do?

See your doctor so he or she can perform tests to determine whether or not you have diabetes or prediabetes.
Your doctor will make recommendations to bring down your blood sugar level to treat diabetes or so that you can
delay the development of diabetes.
