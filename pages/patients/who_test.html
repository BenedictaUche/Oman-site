Who should be tested for diabetes & prediabetes

Who should be tested for diabetes
& prediabetes

There are several groups of people who should be tested for prediabetes and diabetes:

  • Adults aged 45 and older

    The risk of developing prediabetes and type 2 diabetes increases with age,
    so it is recommended that adults in this age group get tested every 3 years.

  • People with high blood pressure

    High blood pressure is a risk factor for prediabetes and type 2 diabetes,
    so individuals with high blood pressure should be tested.

  • People with abnormal cholesterol levels

    Abnormal cholesterol levels, such as high levels of triglycerides or low levels
    of high-density lipoprotein (HDL) cholesterol, can increase the risk of prediabetes
    and type 2 diabetes.

  • People with a family history of prediabetes or type 2 diabetes

    If you have a family history of these conditions, you may be at an increased
    risk and should be tested.

  • People who are overweight or obese

    Being overweight or obese increases the risk of prediabetes and type 2 diabetes.

  • Women who have had gestational diabetes

    Women who have had gestational diabetes during pregnancy are at an increased risk
    of developing prediabetes and type 2 diabetes later in life.

It is important for individuals in these groups to speak with a healthcare provider about
getting tested for prediabetes and type 2 diabetes. Early detection and treatment can help prevent
the development of serious health complications.

If i have signs, what should i do?

See your doctor so he or she can perform tests to determine whether or not you have diabetes or prediabetes.
Your doctor will make recommendations to bring down your blood sugar level to treat diabetes or so that you can
delay the development of diabetes.
