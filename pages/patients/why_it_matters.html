Why Diabetes matters

Why Diabetes matters

Diabetes is a major health concern affecting more than 425 million people worldwide,
including over a million children and adolescents under 20 years old. It is especially prevalent
among people over 65 years old, with one-third of them living with diabetes. If no action is taken,
the number of people with diabetes is projected to rise to 629 million in 2045.

However, it is worth noting that some high-income countries have recently seen a decline in diabetes
incidence. In addition, there are 352 million people with impaired glucose tolerance who are at high
risk of developing diabetes. It is critical to take urgent action to improve diabetes outcomes and reduce
the global burden of this disease.
You can read more about the prevalence of diabetes

Diabetes, if left untreated, can cause damage to several major organs in the body and lead to serious
complications. The longer you have diabetes and the harder it is to control your blood sugar, the greater
the risk of these complications, which may include:

  1. Cardiovascular disease

    Diabetes can increase the risk of a condition called atherosclerosis, which is the
    hardening and narrowing of the walls of the arteries. This can lead to a variety of
    cardiovascular problems, including angina, heart attack, stroke, and peripheral
    arterial disease, which can potentially lead to amputation of limbs due to gangrene.
    It is important for individuals with diabetes to take steps to manage their condition
    and maintain good cardiovascular health to reduce their risk of these complications.

  2. Kidney disease (Nephropathy)

    Longstanding and uncontrolled diabetes can lead to irreversible end-stage kidney
    disease(kidney failure) , which may require dialysis or a kidney transplant.

  3. Nerve damage (Neuropathy)

    Diabetes can lead to damage of the tiny blood vessels that supply the nerves in the limbs,
    particularly the legs. This damage can cause tingling, numbness, and burning sensations that
    typically start at the tips of the toes or fingers and gradually move upwards. If left untreated,
    this damage can result in the loss of all sensation, which can increase the risk of foot ulcers and amputation.
    The nerves of the digestive system may also be affected, leading to symptoms such as nausea, vomiting, diarrhea,
    or constipation. Additionally, nerve damage caused by diabetes can cause erectile dysfunction in men.
    It is important for individuals with diabetes to carefully manage their condition and receive appropriate treatment
    to help prevent or minimize these complications.

  4. Eye damage (Retinopathy)

    Diabetes can lead to damage of the tiny blood vessels that supply the nerves in the limbs,
    particularly the legs. This damage can cause tingling, numbness, and burning sensations that
    typically start at the tips of the toes or fingers and gradually move upwards. If left untreated,

    The nerves of the digestive system may also be affected, leading to symptoms such as nausea, vomiting, diarrhea,
    or constipation. Additionally, nerve damage caused by diabetes can cause erectile dysfunction in men.
    It is important for individuals with diabetes to carefully manage their condition and receive appropriate
    treatment to help prevent or minimize these complications.

  5. Skin and Mouth conditions

    Diabetes is a risk for bacterial and fungal skin infections as well as other skin disorders.
    Poor dental hygiene is a risk factor for mouth infections.

If i have signs, what should i do?

See your doctor so he or she can perform tests to determine whether or not you have diabetes or prediabetes.
Your doctor will make recommendations to bring down your blood sugar level to treat diabetes or so that you can
delay the development of diabetes.
