Home Monitoring

Monitoring blood glucose levels is an important aspect of managing diabetes.
This can be done through self-monitoring at home using a glucose meter.

Here are some tips for effective home monitoring:

  1. Follow your healthcare provider's recommendations for how often to check
    your blood glucose levels. This may be before and after meals, before and
    after physical activity, or at specific times of day.
  2. Use a clean, sterile lancet to prick your finger and obtain a small drop of
    blood for testing.
  3. Use a clean, accurate glucose meter to measure your blood glucose levels.
    Follow the manufacturer's instructions for use.
  4. Record your blood glucose levels in a logbook or use a diabetes management
    app to track your results over time.
  5. Share your blood glucose levels with your healthcare provider at your appointments.
    This will help them adjust your treatment plan if needed.
  6. Check for patterns in your blood glucose levels. For example, do your levels tend to
    be higher after certain meals or activities? This information can help you make lifestyle
    changes to better manage your diabetes.

It's important to remember that self-monitoring is just one aspect of diabetes
management. It's also important to follow a healthy diet, get regular physical
activity, and take any prescribed medications as directed. Working with your healthcare
team is key to managing your diabetes effectively.

If i have signs, what should i do?

See your doctor so he or she can perform tests to determine whether or not you have diabetes or prediabetes.
Your doctor will make recommendations to bring down your blood sugar level to treat diabetes or so that you can
delay the development of diabetes.
