Prediabetes

Prediabetes is a condition in which an individual's blood sugar levels are higher than normal,
but not high enough to be classified as type 2 diabetes. It is often a precursor to type 2 diabetes,
which is a chronic condition that occurs when the body is unable to properly regulate blood sugar levels.

People with prediabetes are at an increased risk of developing type 2 diabetes, as well as other serious
health conditions such as heart disease and stroke. It is important for individuals with prediabetes to
take steps to lower their risk of developing these conditions by making lifestyle changes such as eating
a healthy diet, getting regular physical activity, and maintaining a healthy weight.

Prediabetes can often be reversed with lifestyle changes, but it is important to be proactive and make
these changes before the condition progresses to diabetes. If left unmanaged, prediabetes can lead to
serious health complications and shortened lifespan.

It is important for individuals to get their blood sugar levels checked regularly to detect prediabetes
early on and take steps to manage it. This may involve working with a healthcare provider to develop a
treatment plan that includes lifestyle changes and possibly medication.

Overall, prediabetes is a serious condition that requires proactive management to prevent the development
of type 2 diabetes and other serious health complications.

If i have signs, what should i do?

See your doctor so he or she can perform tests to determine whether or not you have diabetes or prediabetes.
Your doctor will make recommendations to bring down your blood sugar level to treat diabetes or so that you can
delay the development of diabetes.
